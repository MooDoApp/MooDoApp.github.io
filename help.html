Quick Help
Super Quick Start
Start by typing in the white space in your Data Pane (the one with blue buttons). After adding a few items, right click on one of them and explore your options. You can control what appears on your Timeline Pane (the one with red buttons) by adding Dates to items. Explore the tips below to gain a better understanding of how to use Moo.do.
Creating Items - [Enter]
To create new items just press enter. Moo.do is designed to work like your favorite text editor, think of each line as a different item.
More Info

There are a few different ways to create new items or get your data into Moo.do, but enter is the most common way. You may also use the button at the top of each pane to add an item to the top of your list.

If you have a large amount of data that you need to import from your email, another app, or some content from a website, you can paste [Ctrl + V] it anywhere you'd like. If you have a bunch of data stuck in another app Moo.do may support importing it directly. Use the button in the top left to open the sidebar and select Import Data in the Options Menu.

Deleting Items - [None]
You can use either the Backspace or Delete keys to delete an item. Additionally you may delete an item through the right-click menu.
Indenting Items - [Tab]
You can change the hierarchy of your items either by pressing Tab or through the right click menu.
More Info

Changing the hierarchy of an item is in is essential to keeping your information organized. You can create individual lists of tasks under a single header or keep your notes on one subject grouped together. Having a properly structured document allows you to zoom in or out and only focus on the parts that you need.

Unindenting Items - [Shift + Tab]
You can change the hierarchy of your items either by pressing Shift + Tab or through the right click menu.
More Info

@ Dates
Add dates to items by typing @date;. This will add an entry in your timeline and highlight the date.
More Info
+ Contacts
Once you have enabled contact syncing through the menu in the sidebar [], you can add contacts to items by typing +name. This allows you to quickly access their contact information on the go.
More Info
Find/Search - [Ctrl + F]
The search bar is at the top of the pane next to the icon. You can use search to drill down and only view the items that match your search.
More Info

If you know some of the content for an item you're looking for, you don't have to remember where you put it in your document. By using the search box at the top of each pane you can quickly find and jump to the data you are looking for. This is done by removing all data that does not match your search and only showing you relevant information.

!!! Set Item Priority - [Ctrl + 1|2|3]
You can set priorities on items to have them appear in your Timeline Pane.
More Info

Moo.do supports setting three different priority levels and no-priority. Any item with a priority will be added to your Timeline Panes along with your items with due dates.

Access the Outline - [Ctrl + O]
The outline can be used to quickly change the section of your document you are looking at.
More Info

The outline is a great way to jump between sections of your document without worrying about scrolling. This is especially useful in documents with a lot of data. There is an outline button at the top left of each pane next to the icon.

Open the Sidebar - [None]
Open the sidebar by pressing the icon in the top left to access your settings and documents.
More Info

The sidebar contains your settings, your documents, documents that are shared with you and menus to import and export your data from Moo.do. You can read more about using multiple documents in the advanced help section.

Flag an Item - [Ctrl + ']
You can mark items that you or someone else need to look at again the next time you open the app.
More Info

Flagging is similar to priority in that it is searchable and visually changes the look of an item. An item may be both flagged and prioritized.

Zoom In - [Ctrl + Enter]
Change your view by focusing on exactly what you want to see.
More Info

Each pane is focusable on a single item, by default this is the root or home item. If you have a large amount of data in your document, it is sometimes useful to only look at a smaller section of that data. If you are planning two separate projects, seeing data for both isn't going to be helpful. Zooming is an easy way to reduce the amount of extra clutter you have to sort through.

Zooming may also be done through the outline by clicking on a title.

Zoom Out - [Ctrl + Shift + Enter]
Expand your view to see more information.
More Info

Once you've zoomed in, you need a good way to get back out to see the rest of your data again. You may zoom out either using a hotkey or clicking the blue buttons at the top of the pane.

Zooming may also be done through the outline by clicking on a title.

Collapse - [Ctrl + Comma]
Hide an item and all items underneath it.
More Info

Sometimes you have lists that aren't accessed that often or you want to hide for a while. Collapsing a list hides all of its sub-items.

Reorder Items - [Click + Drag]
You can change the location of your items by clicking the left side of the item and dragging it. Move the mouse left until you see the grabber hand, click and drag.

Answers
I just started using Moo.do, how do I add data?

You can either enter your data manually or use the Data Import button to bring your data in from other services. You may also copy and paste almost any data on the web, your email, or other apps into Moo.do.

Now that I've got my data in Moo.do how should I organize it?

Moo.do doesn't force an organizational system on you, you're free to organize however you'd like. We do have a couple tips for making the most out of the features that Moo.do offers you though. If you would still like a suggestion on how to keep your data organized check out our blog.

What are panes? Why is one pane red and one blue?

Each pane is a different way of viewing your data. By default there are two panes available, one Data Pane and one Timeline Pane. The Data Panes have blue title bars and are editable. This is where you do all of your data input and organization. The timeline panes have red title bars and show all of your items that have a priority or dates. You can add or remove panes at any time using the Pane Management buttons at the top right of the page.

Advanced Help - Top
Searching

Search is a powerful tool to ensure you can find exactly what you're looking for. You can search for plain words in each pane, or search based on priority (!, !!, !!!), flagged (''), and completion status (//). You may also negate (-) each search term.

For example - to search for items tagged with your name, that have not been completed and have a priority you do #tag -// !.

The Outline

The outline contains an interactive list of all the items that contain sub-items. This is the skeleton of your document and allows you to quickly switch between different items instead of scrolling through your entire document. If the outline is still too long, you may collapse individual items in it by using the buttons.

Dates

Adding dates to items ensures that they show up in your Timeline Panes. Additionally you will receive notifications on your mobile devices before your items are scheduled. Add a date to your item by typing @ and then the date you want. You can use @now, @soon, @later, @today, @tomorrow in addition to most date formats like @friday 3:00pm or @5/26 9:45am.

Contacts

You can directly reference your contacts through Moo.do by doing +name in the text of an item. By default Moo.do does not import your contacts information, so to enable this feature you need to go to the menu and check the Sync Contacts button. If you have not previously given Moo.do permission to access your contacts you will see a prompt asking you to allow access. If you are running an aggressive popup blocker and don't see a prompt, check to see if it was blocked.

You contacts in Moo.do are updated automatically at midnight each day, if you need any new contacts loaded before then, open settings back up and press the Refresh Contacts button.

Pane Management

By default you have two panes opened, one Data Pane and one Timeline Pane. The data pane has blue buttons and the timeline pane has red buttons. Occasionally it is useful to have more than two panes, or more than one pane of a certain type. At the top-right of the screen there are buttons to add new panes. adds a new Data Pane and adds a new Timeline Pane. Additionally in the top right corner of each pane there is a button to switch the mode of the current pane.

If you have opened too many panes or just want to see less information at once, you can close each pane by pressing the icon in the top-right of the pane.

Timeline Panes

Timeline panes have red buttons and show you a day-by-day breakdown of items that you have completed and upcoming items with dates.To add items to the timeline pane, insert a date into the text of an item by doing @date. The entire pane is not editable, but you can jump to the editable version of the item by double clicking the item you are interested in.

Timeline panes have an additional function which is to show Archived items. These are generally items you have completed in the past and wanted hidden from your current view as they are no longer relevant. If you want a full history of items you have completed by date, press the icon in the top-right of the pane.

Multiple Documents and Sharing

Having multiple documents allows you to have control at a higher level over how much data you have to sort through at once. Each document may also be shared with other people, so having a document to collaborate with your friends, family or co-workers is easily doable while keeping your private data to yourself.

Add new documents by opening the sidebar and pressing the button next to Documents at the top of the pane. You can change the title of the document and who has access to it using the and menus on the right of each document.

Import Data

Your data should be portable between services. If you have data stuck in another service and want to get it into Moo.do, you can use the Import menu. This is located in the Sidebar under the Options menu. Currently Moo.do supports direct import from Wunderlist, Google Tasks and Workflowy. Additionally any other service that can export to OPML/XML or be copy/pasted can be easily imported.

If there is another service that you use that does not easily export data, send us an email at support@moo.do.
