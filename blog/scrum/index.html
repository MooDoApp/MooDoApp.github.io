Moo.do for Scrum
Written by Jay Meistrich on September 08, 2014 in ideas

Many friends of Moo.do like to organize their projects on a Scrum (or Kanban) board for Agile Development. This continues our series of examples of how people and teams are using Moo.do with a description of how Moo.do can be used as a Scrum board. While we appreciate the physicality of plastering notes on a wall, we think powerful software is much more effective and less messy.

Organization

First let's set up the sections of the board in outline form. We use Todo, In Progress, Testing, and Complete to describe the phases of each task. You could also subdivide your sections, like Bugs and Features for a software project.

This outline format is a great way to read through everything, but it gets hard to read when there's a lot of items in each list. So let's spread them out by adding more panes, with one focused on each list.

This is a fully interactive instance of Moo.do. Try dragging items between panes by grabbing the space to the left of an item.

It's easy to visualize how much work is left in each phase and drag and drop items between panes as we make progress. Now that the basics are set up, let's look at what else Moo.do can do!

Assign tasks

Our team assigns tasks using a #tag for each person. I use Moo.do's search to view only my tasks or see what others on my team are working on.

See what's important

"What Do I Do Next Paralysis" can be a big problem when faced with a lot of tasks. Being able to quickly prioritize using Moo.do's starring and three priority levels makes it easy to always know what's next and what needs further discussion. During our meetings, we open an Agenda pane and talk through starred items and check up on high priority tasks. We can even focus and search the Agenda.

My to-do list

I use Moo.do's' powerful search to focus on just what I need.

!!! -//Important and incomplete items
#Grant //What Grant has finished
#iPhoneWhat needs to be fixed for iPhone
#Feedback -#Unassigned feedback

It's great to have an overview of what everyone is doing, but it's very distracting (and scary!) to stare at it all day long. I check the overall list in the morning or evening and spend the day focused on my own tasks by searching for only tasks assigned to me, or even only my highest priority tasks.

Show a dashboard on a TV

Many people like to have a scrum board on a wall in a central location that everyone can see. Moo.do is synchronized in real time as you type, so with Moo.do on a TV in the office, you'll see tasks getting moved around and completed as people work on them. You could have one pane showing what's happening in the "In Progress" section, one showing an agenda of the highest priority tasks, and one tracking finished tasks in the "Complete" section.
