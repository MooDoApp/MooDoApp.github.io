Public Beta
Written by Jay Meistrich and Grant Watters on June 11, 2014 in updates

We are proud to announce that Moo.do is entering into public beta today - you can signup at http://moo.do. Two years ago we found ourselves and our friends frustrated with the difficulty of organizing and prioritizing multiple personal projects alongside work. We started working towards creating a tool that could be both powerful and easy to use.

Throughout the development process we have been using Moo.do as our only tool for planning, bug tracking, communication and release tracking. At the same time we have been using it to manage other projects, travel plans, and our day-to-day lives. Our current beta testers have been able to switch to using Moo.do as their primary organization system. Today we are happy to bring the power and simplicity of Moo.do to everyone.

Moo.do lets you organize your life as easily as writing on a piece of paper. It is flexible, available offline, it works on your mobile devices, and synchronizes in realtime with all your devices and other collaborators. Moo.do organizes your projects, to do lists, calendar, contacts, reminders, events, and anything else you can write down. You can even share specific lists with your friends, group members or co-workers.

As of today Moo.do is accessible to anyone by opening http://moo.do in a browser on your computer, iPhone, iPad, or Android device. In the near future native applications will be available for each platform, but you don’t have to wait for them as the web version is fully featured.

Moo.do will help you organize your life and then keep you on track. If you have feedback, questions, or would like to request a feature - get in touch, we are excited to help you out.
